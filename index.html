About My Approach

Many individuals seeking psychological support often find themselves contending with unwanted thoughts, feelings, or behaviours, frequently referred to as 'symptoms'. These symptoms often surface as adaptive responses to earlier challenges, particularly when other options may have been scarce.

One of the remarkable faculties of our minds is the ability to encode these solutions into our implicit memory, automating them into what we might call our 'autopilot'. While this can be advantageous, facilitating numerous tasks without requiring much conscious effort, these once adaptive patterns may feel more like obstacles than solutions over time, or as our circumstances evolve.

Rather than categorising these patterns as 'bad' and striving to suppress them, my therapeutic approach focuses on recognising the value they either once had or may still have in your life. I allow space for understanding and curiosity to develop, so that we can explore both the origins and the profound emotional significance of these patterns in your life. Take the example of a relentless inner critic. While it may be presently overwhelming, it could have originally served as a protective mechanism to anticipate and manage others' reactions.

Every decision we make carries advantages and drawbacks, which may not be entirely visible when initially taken, given our varying emotional states and changing life circumstances. For example, at some point, you may have chosen to dodge discomfort, only to find that this choice eventually led to feelings of loneliness or emptiness.

Such choices might have been made by a part of your mind in the past, preserved in implicit memory, and subsequently forgotten, resulting in the conscious mind feeling at odds with the unconscious/implicit mind. This dynamic might resemble two teams within a company working against each other.

While we can attempt to understand these components of our mind intellectually, genuine resolution of internal conflict often arises when we engage with these parts on an emotional level. This involves progressing beyond a purely logical grasp of a perspective and cultivating a deep, embodied understanding. This depth of comprehension can nurture a more compassionate and nuanced relationship with these aspects of ourselves, fostering more fruitful internal collaboration. The end product of this process often entails either developing a greater tolerance for the inner mechanics of the mind, or a re-assessment and adjustment of past decisions on an emotional level.

Through therapy, the goal is to surface these unconscious decisions into conscious awareness, shedding light on the choices various aspects of yourself made in the past. Recognising these choices does not mean rejecting these parts, but instead acknowledging them, appreciating their significance, and reconsidering their ongoing role in your life. This fosters an environment of openness, understanding, and harmony between the conscious and unconscious mind.

Enhanced self-awareness opens the door for exploring alternative choices that harmonise better with your current needs and circumstances, or for fostering a deeper acceptance of a choice that continues to be beneficial. As your therapist, my role is to guide you on this journey towards acknowledging and integrating all aspects of your mind, with the ultimate aim of improving your mental health and overall well-being.
