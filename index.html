Many people seeking psychological support find themselves grappling with unwanted thoughts, feelings, or compulsions/behaviors, what we often call 'symptoms.' These symptoms typically emerge as solutions to early challenges, especially when better options are unavailable. Over time, these thought patterns can become automatic, and when our circumstances change, they can start to feel more like obstacles than solutions.

Rather than labeling these patterns as 'bad' and trying to suppress them, my approach to therapy aims to uncover their origins and understand the value they once had in your life. Think of a harsh inner critic, for example. While its constant criticism may feel overwhelming, it might have started as a way to predict and prepare for how others may react to you.

By understanding these symptoms and the role they've played, we can then explore new ways to meet the same needs. Maybe you have tried to silence your inner critic completely, but are finding this inner conflict exhausting. Sometimes, understanding your inner critic can open the door to collaborating with it more harmoniously.

My aim is to guide you towards greater self-awareness and an understanding of your symptoms, helping you appreciate the value of your thought patterns, and supporting you in finding balance in your life. This journey allows you to embrace and integrate all aspects of your mind, leading to improved mental health and well-being.

Psychological Services
